# Boss: Vorath the Unmade

## Act
- Act 3

## Role
- Chaos and physical hybrid, act 3 pre-final encounter

## Encounter Summary
- Vorath the Unmade is a destabilizing encounter where the arena itself partially dissolves at each phase threshold.
- At 60% HP, one arena quadrant disappears. At 30% HP, a second quadrant goes.
- Players must adapt to shrinking safe area while handling a chaotic mixed-damage kit.

## Key Mechanics (Quick List)
- Unmaking Slash: wide horizontal melee arc covering 180 degrees in front.
- Void Geyser: delayed upward burst at random arena position.
- Chaos Tendrils: five tendrils spreading in arc from boss, lingering for 2 seconds.
- Dissolution (Phase Threshold): removes arena quadrant at 60% and 30% HP.
- Final Unmake (Phase 3): boss splits into two mirrored clones, each with 35% of original HP.

## Phase Flow
- Phase 1 (100% to 60%): full arena, standard kit.
- First Dissolution (60%): first quadrant removed, arena shrinks.
- Phase 2 (60% to 30%): reduced arena, Void Geysers become more frequent.
- Second Dissolution (30%): second quadrant removed, remaining arena is half original size.
- Phase 3: Final Unmake splits Vorath into two clones.

## Core Mechanics

### Unmaking Slash
- 180-degree forward arc melee.
- Cannot be outranged; covers significant area.
- Counter: position at diagonal flank; the 90-degree dead zones on each side are safe.

### Void Geyser
- Ground marker appears, burst fires upward after 1.4 seconds.
- Deals physical and chaos mixed damage.
- Multiple geysers can appear simultaneously from Phase 2.
- Counter: exit marker immediately; the 1.4-second window is sufficient.

### Chaos Tendrils
- Five tendrils spread in a 90-degree arc from the boss.
- Linger for 2 seconds after landing; stepping into lingering tendril deals moderate chaos damage.
- Counter: dodge the initial spread, then navigate around lingering tendril positions.

### Dissolution (Phase Thresholds)
- At 60% HP: northeast quadrant of arena becomes void and impassable.
- At 30% HP: southwest quadrant becomes void.
- Counter: mentally remap safe zone after each dissolution; adjust positioning immediately.

### Final Unmake (Phase 3)
- Vorath splits into two clones with 35% HP each.
- Clones share the same ability kit.
- Killing one clone first is recommended; both clones being low HP simultaneously is dangerous.
- Counter: focus one clone, then clean up the second.

## Telegraphs And Counters
- Blade glow before Unmaking Slash: move to diagonal flank.
- Ground shimmer before Void Geyser: exit marker.
- Body roll animation before Chaos Tendrils: prepare to dodge spread.
- Arena edge darkening indicates upcoming Dissolution quadrant.

## Positioning Rules
- After each Dissolution, spend the first few seconds remapping safe area.
- In Phase 3, separate clones by targeting one from the arena edge to increase distance from the other.
- Never retreat into a corner; Dissolution may eliminate corner safety.

## Common Failure Modes
- Panicking during Dissolution and running into the newly voided quadrant.
- Staying in Phase 3 center and taking combined clone abilities from both sides.
- Not anticipating second Dissolution at 30% and being out of position.

## Punish Windows
- After Unmaking Slash: 1.9 seconds from diagonal flank.
- After Void Geyser burst resolves: 1.5 seconds.
- After Chaos Tendrils linger expires: 2.0 seconds.
- After clone kill in Phase 3: 3.5 seconds before survivor clone resumes.

- Second Dissolution removes the southwest quadrant at 30% HP.
- Final Unmake clones each have exactly 35% of Vorath's original HP.
