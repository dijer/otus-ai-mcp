# Path of Exile 2: Boss Phase Window Mastery

## Part 1: Why Phase Windows Matter

### 1.1 Damage Windows

Every boss in Path of Exile 2 creates periods where the boss is vulnerable and periods where the boss is dangerous. The basic goal is simple: do not waste burst during invulnerability, and do not greedily stand in danger while a phase transition is about to begin.

**Core phase window pattern**:
1. Boss begins visible animation.
2. Boss commits to an attack or movement.
3. Player can safely deal damage during the recovery portion.
4. Boss enters transition or defensive state.
5. Damage becomes inefficient or impossible.

**Practical meaning**:
- If your build has 10,000 burst DPS, but you apply it during invulnerability, effective DPS is 0.
- If your build has 5,000 sustained DPS and you maintain uptime for 80% of the fight, effective combat output is 4,000 DPS.
- If your build learns the phase timer and aligns burst with windows, the same build can reach 6,000-7,000 effective DPS without changing gear.

### 1.2 Window Types

**Short window**:
- Duration: 1-2 seconds
- Typical use: quick punish after dodge
- Best for: instant casts, melee follow-up, minion resummon
- Risk: high if you overcommit

**Medium window**:
- Duration: 3-5 seconds
- Typical use: full rotation of a balanced build
- Best for: channel skills, two-step combos, posture resets
- Risk: moderate

**Long window**:
- Duration: 6-10 seconds
- Typical use: boss stagger, scripted vulnerability, post-transition punish
- Best for: burst cooldowns, multi-layer DOT refresh, gem swap setups
- Risk: low if mechanics are understood

### 1.3 Window Quality Score

A useful manual score for each fight:

- 0 points: no window, pure survival phase.
- 1 point: 1-2 second punish.
- 2 points: 3-5 second punish.
- 3 points: 6+ second punish.
- 4 points: multiple windows per cycle.
- 5 points: windows align with cooldowns and flask uptime.

If a boss scores below 2, burst builds lose value and sustained builds gain value.

## Part 2: Universal Boss Phase Model

### 2.1 Standard Three-Phase Structure

Most major bosses follow a three-part pattern:

**Phase A: Opening pressure**
- Boss uses basic attacks.
- Player learns safe positioning.
- Damage is acceptable but not yet optimal.
- Common mistake: spending all cooldowns immediately.

**Phase B: Controlled escalation**
- Boss adds hazards, summons, or forced movement.
- Arena becomes less safe.
- Player must stop greed and respect telegraphs.
- Common mistake: standing still to finish a cast.

**Phase C: Execution or enrage**
- Boss becomes more aggressive or enters script-heavy sequence.
- Some attacks chain into one another.
- Player must decide whether to burn phase or reset position.
- Common mistake: assuming the boss is almost dead and ignoring mechanics.

### 2.2 Transition Signals

Recognizing transition signals is the fastest way to improve boss kills.

Common signals:
- Boss stops moving for half a second.
- Arena lighting changes.
- Voice line triggers.
- Boss raises weapon or disappears.
- Secondary enemies spawn.
- Floor effect appears in a ring or line.

Interpretation rules:
- If the boss is still attacking, do not greed burst unless you have explicit immunity.
- If the boss has no target lock and is repositioning, the next attack is usually scripted.
- If the arena changes color, a new phase is usually starting within 1-2 seconds.

### 2.3 Burn vs Respect Decision

Use this decision tree during every transition:

1. Is the boss below 20% HP?
- Yes: consider burn if you know the final script.
- No: respect the transition.

2. Is your burst cooldown ready?
- Yes: hold it until the first safe window.
- No: use sustained damage and save defensive resources.

3. Do you know the next mechanic?
- Yes: punish immediately after the mechanic ends.
- No: move first, ask questions later.

## Part 3: Boss Family Patterns

### 3.1 Aggressive Melee Bosses

These bosses have high contact pressure and short recovery.

Common traits:
- Fast gap closers.
- Cone slashes.
- Leap attacks.
- Short stun windows after missed swings.

Strategy:
- Keep lateral movement.
- Punish after leap landing.
- Do not stand directly in front unless the attack is already committed.
- Save movement skill for the second swing, not the first.

Window profile:
- Average punish window: 1.5-2.5 seconds.
- Effective playstyle: hit-and-run.
- Best builds: high mobility melee, ranged kite, traps, instant cast spells.

### 3.2 Spellcaster Bosses

These bosses are slower but more punishing if you ignore line of sight or area control.

Common traits:
- Delayed projectiles.
- Ground effects.
- Summoned hazards.
- Channel interruptions.

Strategy:
- Break line of sight when possible.
- Move perpendicular to projectile lines.
- Punish after channel start or channel end.
- Avoid greed during ground effect placement.

Window profile:
- Average punish window: 3-5 seconds.
- Effective playstyle: consistent DPS, area denial, ranged burst.
- Best builds: totems, minions, long-range spells.

### 3.3 Summoner Bosses

These bosses create density and force target prioritization.

Common traits:
- Minion waves.
- Explosive adds.
- Healing interactions.
- Arena clutter.

Strategy:
- Identify whether adds are resources or threats.
- If adds enable boss buffs, kill them immediately.
- If adds are weak and numerous, use area damage to clear them during boss recovery.
- Do not tunnel the boss if the room is filling with hazards.

Window profile:
- Average punish window: 2-4 seconds between add cycles.
- Effective playstyle: hybrid clear plus single-target.
- Best builds: splash damage, corpse exploitation, chain skills.

### 3.4 Multi-Form Bosses

These bosses transform and often reset pressure patterns.

Common traits:
- Distinct phase identities.
- Health breakpoints that trigger form changes.
- Different attack types per form.
- Sometimes full heal or partial reset.

Strategy:
- Save major cooldowns for form 2 or form 3 if the first form is easy.
- Learn whether transitions preserve debuffs or cleanse them.
- If the boss heals on transition, push harder before the breakpoint.

Window profile:
- Average punish window: variable, depends on form.
- Effective playstyle: flexible rotation planning.
- Best builds: versatile kits with multiple damage modes.

## Part 4: Encounter Planning by Build Type

### 4.1 Burst Builds

Burst builds care most about whether the boss has a repeatable vulnerability cycle.

Burst checklist:
- Is the vulnerability predictable?
- Is the boss stationary long enough?
- Does the boss become immune after a threshold?
- Can you store resources until the correct moment?

If the answer to all four is yes, burst builds perform exceptionally.
If the answer to two or more is no, burst builds become inefficient.

**Example**:
- Burst build total cooldown package: 15,000 damage over 4 seconds.
- If the boss gives only 2 seconds of real opening, effective burst = 7,500 damage.
- If you can force a 6 second opening by baiting the attack correctly, effective burst = 15,000 damage.

### 4.2 Sustained Damage Builds

Sustained builds care less about perfect windows and more about uptime.

Sustained checklist:
- Can you keep attacking while moving?
- Does your damage survive interruptions?
- Do you lose too much when repositioning?
- Does the boss frequently stop and start?

If the boss is highly mobile or has frequent interrupts, sustained builds usually outperform burst builds.

**Example**:
- Sustained build output: 4,000 DPS.
- Real uptime during fight: 85%.
- Effective damage: 3,400 DPS.
- If the boss has 20% downtime but burst build has 50% wasted damage, sustained build wins.

### 4.3 Hybrid Builds

Hybrid builds are strongest in blind progression because they can handle both short punish windows and extended phases.

Hybrid checklist:
- Does the build have a basic rotation plus a cooldown burst?
- Can the build survive while waiting for burst?
- Can the build swap between clear and single-target without losing identity?

These builds often feel less explosive but are more consistent over many bosses.

## Part 5: Practical Fight Execution

### 5.1 Pre-Fight Setup

Before entering a boss arena, verify:
- Flask charges are full.
- Movement skill is off cooldown.
- Burst cooldowns are ready.
- Summons are active if your build uses them.
- Defensive buffs are applied.
- You know the boss opening sequence.

If any of these are false, your first window is already weakened.

### 5.2 First 10 Seconds

The first 10 seconds determine the rest of the encounter.

Recommended sequence:
1. Enter carefully.
2. Identify the opening move.
3. Dodge first, damage second.
4. Save burst if the boss is immediately transitioning.
5. Only commit major resources once the boss has shown the first stable pattern.

Common early-fight failure:
- Player sees the boss and uses every cooldown instantly.
- Boss transitions early.
- Half the burst is wasted.
- The rest of the fight becomes slower and more dangerous.

### 5.3 Final 20%

Many fights become harder in the last 20%.

Why:
- Bosses often add speed.
- Players become greedy.
- Healing or utility windows disappear.
- Resource pools are smaller.

Final phase rules:
- Stop pretending the boss is already dead.
- Hold defensive flask if you know an enrage is coming.
- Keep movement discipline.
- If a safe window exists, use your strongest damage now rather than later.

## Part 6: Example Boss Planning Templates

### 6.1 Fast Melee Boss Template

- Opening: move sideways, bait leap.
- Punish: attack after landing.
- Mid phase: stay near edge, avoid front cone.
- Final phase: save movement skill for double swing.
- Window score: 2/5.
- Best build: mobile melee or ranged.

### 6.2 Arena Control Boss Template

- Opening: clear hazards first if they block movement.
- Punish: attack after channel or summon cycle.
- Mid phase: rotate around safe tiles.
- Final phase: avoid corners unless forced.
- Window score: 3/5.
- Best build: sustained ranged or totem.

### 6.3 Multi-Transition Boss Template

- Opening: do not spend everything.
- First transition: identify whether boss cleanses.
- Second transition: pre-position for the next arena state.
- Final phase: use burst only when the pattern repeats and the boss is vulnerable.
- Window score: 4/5.
- Best build: hybrid or burst with reserved cooldowns.

## Part 7: Boss Encounter Mistakes

### 7.1 Greed Mistakes

- Greed 1: casting one more attack instead of moving.
- Greed 2: using burst on invulnerable target.
- Greed 3: chasing a low-HP boss into a lethal mechanic.
- Greed 4: ignoring add waves because boss is "almost dead".

### 7.2 Knowledge Mistakes

- Not knowing the transition signal.
- Not knowing which attack is safe to stand near.
- Not knowing which phase has the strongest punish.
- Not knowing whether debuffs persist between forms.

### 7.3 Execution Mistakes

- Panic movement with no purpose.
- Standing still when the boss has already committed to burst.
- Using the wrong cooldown in the wrong phase.
- Forgetting to refresh auras or summons before the window.

---

## Conclusion

Boss phase window mastery is the difference between a messy fight and a clean kill. The best players do not merely react faster. They understand when the boss is vulnerable, when it is about to transition, and when damage is worth spending.

The pattern is always the same: learn the signal, respect the transition, punish the window, repeat until the fight ends.
