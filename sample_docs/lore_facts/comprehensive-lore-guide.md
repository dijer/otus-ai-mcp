# Path of Exile 2: Complete Lore and World-Building Reference

## Part 1: World Overview and Geography

### 1.1 The Continent Map

**Wraeclast** (primary continent):
- Area 1: Coast (acts 1-2 gateway)
- Area 2: Forest (acts 2-3 transition)
- Area 3: Mountains (acts 3-4 endgame hub)
- Area 4: Underground (acts 4-5 deep zones)
- Area 5: Capital City (act 6 culmination)
- Area 6: Void Realm (theoretical endgame space, not yet added)

**Size**: 200 square kilometers (estimated)

**Population**: 
- Pre-cataclysm: 5 million
- Post-cataclysm: 10,000 (survivors)
- Hostile creatures: billions (monsters outnumber humans 1,000:1)

### 1.2 Major Factions

**The Ascendancy** (player-aligned):
- Origin: mysterious organization
- Goal: stabilize world, defeat elder gods
- Strength: player characters (you)
- Weakness: limited numbers

**The Templars** (church-like faction):
- Origin: ancient religious organization
- Goal: control divine power, deny prophecies
- Strength: organization, resources, magic
- Weakness: dogmatic, slow to adapt

**The Sorcerers** (rogue mages):
- Origin: breakaway templar faction
- Goal: control elemental magic, transcend flesh
- Strength: spellcasting, innovation
- Weakness: arrogant, infighting

**The Beast (primordial entity)**:
- Origin: older than time
- Goal: consume world, infinite hunger
- Strength: unkillable (multiple forms)
- Weakness: can be sealed, not destroyed

### 1.3 Lore Progression by Act

**Acts 1-2**: Survival and Escape
- Story: player washes ashore, must escape coastal regions
- Antagonist: corrupted beasts, templar guards
- Resolution: reach inland, escape immediate danger

**Acts 3-4**: Ascendancy Discovery
- Story: player discovers ascendancy power within
- Antagonist: elder guardians, primordial beings
- Resolution: unlock ascendancy, gain power

**Acts 5-6**: Confrontation and Choice
- Story: player confronts final antagonist (Kitava/Elder)
- Antagonist: world-ending entity
- Resolution: defeat or seal threat, world survives (or falls)

## Part 5: Cosmology and Magic System

### 5.1 Magic Types

**Divine Magic** (templar-style):
- Source: faith and belief
- Strength: protection, healing, order
- Weakness: dependent on faith (fails if doubted)

**Elemental Magic** (sorcerer-style):
- Source: command of elements
- Strength: destructive power, versatility
- Weakness: takes time to cast (vulnerable to interruption)

**Chaos Magic** (shadow-style):
- Source: corruption and void
- Strength: penetrating, unstoppable
- Weakness: morally corrupting (attracts attention)

**Physical Magic** (warrior-style):
- Source: body and will
- Strength: reliable, always available
- Weakness: scaling limitations

### 5.2 Ascendancy Power

**Ascendancy Definition**: Awakening of dormant power within
- Source: mysterious, possibly divine
- Activation: triggered by overcoming challenges
- Manifestation: class-specific unique abilities
- Cost: none (free power granted by universe)

**Mechanics**:
- Each class has 6 passive points (48 total per class = 288 total)
- Allocation path: 40 points typical (maximizes specific stat domain)
- Power scaling: +200% stat boost from full allocation

### 5.3 Corruption and Inheritance

**Corruption**: Exposure to primordial power
- Effect: grants abilities but costs sanity/mortality
- Symptom: black veins, uncontrollable power surges
- Cure: mysterious (possibly no cure)

**Inheritance**: Passing corrupted power to next generation
- Method: unknown
- Cost: life force or soul binding
- Benefit: power without direct corruption

**Player Status**: Ambiguous corruption
- Player doesn't appear corrupted
- Yet possesses impossible ascendancy
- Implication: either immune or secretly corrupted
