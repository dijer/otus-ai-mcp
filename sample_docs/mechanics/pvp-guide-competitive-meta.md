# Path of Exile 2: Complete PvP Guide and Competitive Meta

## Part 1: PvP Overview and Formats

### 1.1 PvP Game Modes

**Deathmatch (1v1 Arena)**:
- Format: 1 player vs. 1 player in small enclosed arena
- Duration: first kill wins, no time limit
- Builds allowed: all builds viable
- Gear scaling: equal stat budget enforced (all players have same item level gear)

**Team Deathmatch (3v3 Arena)**:
- Format: 3 players vs. 3 players
- Duration: team with 0 remaining players loses
- Strategy: focus fire high-threat targets, cc vulnerable targets
- Coordination: requires voice chat for optimal callouts

**Battle Royale (16-32 players)**:
- Format: large arena with shrinking safe zone
- Duration: last player standing wins
- Strategy: scavenge loot from fallen players, avoid large groups early
- Emergent gameplay: political alliances form and break

### 1.2 PvP Ranking and Progression

**Competitive Ladder Tiers**:
- Bronze (0-500 rating): casual players, learning builds
- Silver (500-1,500 rating): semi-competitive, optimized builds
- Gold (1,500-2,500 rating): competitive, mastery-level play
- Platinum (2,500-3,500 rating): elite players, 1-2% population
- Diamond (3,500+ rating): top 0.1%, professional players

**Rating System**: Win/loss against rating-scaled opponents:
- Beating higher-rated opponent: +50 rating
- Beating same-rated opponent: +25 rating
- Losing to lower-rated opponent: -50 rating

This creates a self-correcting system where rating converges to true skill level.

### 1.3 PvP Economy and Rewards

**Seasonal Rewards**:
- Bronze tier: 50 chaos at season end
- Silver tier: 200 chaos + unique cosmetic
- Gold tier: 500 chaos + rare cosmetic + title
- Platinum tier: 1,000 chaos + exclusive cosmetic + leaderboard placement
- Diamond tier: 2,000 chaos + legendary cosmetic + sponsorship eligibility

**Prize Pools**: Seasonal tournaments award 10,000-100,000 chaos to top players.

## Part 2: PvP Build Archetypes

### 2.1 One-Shot Burst Builds

**Core Mechanic**: Deal massive damage in short time window, overwhelm opponent's defenses.

**Viable Archetype Examples**:
- Crit Spell Echo (Wizard class): Chain spells deal 3,000+ damage per hit
- Physical Crit Attack (Rogue class): dual-wielding swords, 5,000+ damage per swing
- Poison Stacking (Assassin): 20+ poison stacks in 3 seconds = 5,000+ DPS

**Strengths**:
- Quick kills (2-4 second fights)
- High skill ceiling (complex rotations)
- Dictate fight pace (opponent plays defense)

**Weaknesses**:
- Vulnerable to cc (stun, stagger interrupts burst)
- Low sustain (trade life pool for damage)
- Relies on landing hits (one miss = fight lost)

**Counter Strategy**: CC immune + tank builds (see below)

### 2.2 Tank/CC Immune Builds

**Core Mechanic**: Prioritize survivability, absorb burst damage, counter-attack.

**Viable Archetype Examples**:
- Block Paladin (Knight): 50% block chance, 5,000+ armor
- Stagger Immune Cyclone (Warrior): face-tank forever, leech sustains
- Energy Shield Templar (Templar): 3,000+ ES, recharge mechanics

**Strengths**:
- Survive burst builds (tank through damage)
- Long fights favor defense (sustain outweighs offense)
- Predictable gameplay (less RNG)

**Weaknesses**:
- Slow kills (grinding DPS races)
- Weak to sustained dps (leech not infinite)
- Difficulty against DoT builds (can't mitigate continuous damage)

**Counter Strategy**: Sustained damage > burst (see below)

### 2.3 Sustained DoT Builds

**Core Mechanic**: Apply damage-over-time effects (poison, burn, bleed), attrition warfare.

**Viable Archetype Examples**:
- Poison Assassin (Shadow): apply poison, dodge forever until poison kills
- Bleed Gladiator (Warrior): stack bleed instances, overwhelm defenses
- Chaos DoT Witch (Witch): apply chaos damage, scale with damage multipliers

**Strengths**:
- Ignore armor/defenses (DoT bypasses some mitigations)
- Sustain through pacing (no mana/resource costs after application)
- Outscale opponents (more DoT stacks = exponential damage)

**Weaknesses**:
- Long fights required (DoT takes time to ramp)
- Countered by cleanse (remove DoT stacks)
- Requires application skill (landing multiple hits to stack)

**Counter Strategy**: Cleanse + fast offense (see below)

### 2.4 Kite/Mobility Builds

**Core Mechanic**: Maintain distance, poke damage, avoid direct confrontation.

**Viable Archetype Examples**:
- Projectile Deadeye (Ranger): shoot from distance, stutter-step backward
- Spell Echo Sorcerer (Witch): multi-projectile spells from safety
- Mobile Shadow (Shadow): dart in/out with high movement speed

**Strengths**:
- Control distance (determine fight proximity)
- Avoid ground effects (high mobility)
- Preserve life pool (distance reduces damage taken)

**Weaknesses**:
- Low sustained damage (trading position for safety)
- Vulnerable in enclosed spaces (can't kite)
- Requires mechanical skill (constant repositioning)

**Counter Strategy**: Close distance + cc (see below)

## Part 4: PvP Mechanics and Counter System

### 4.1 CC Abilities

**Stun** (1 second disable):
- Application: taking enough poise damage (stagger same as PvE)
- Counter: stun immunity gear (Kaom's Roots)
- Use: disrupt spell casting, prevent kiting

**Freeze** (0.3-4 second disable):
- Application: cold damage >0 applies freeze
- Counter: freeze immunity gear or very high cold res
- Use: lockdown mobile builds

**Bleed Slow** (movement speed reduction):
- Application: bleed stack reduction (-20% per stack)
- Counter: cleanse (remove bleed stacks)
- Use: slow tank builds to kitable speeds

### 4.2 Cleanse Mechanics

Some builds include "cleanse" abilities that remove debuffs:

**Example Cleanse Gem** (hypothetical):
- Effect: remove all stacks of specific debuff (bleed, poison)
- Cooldown: 3 seconds
- Mana cost: 50

This creates rock-paper-scissors dynamics:
- Burst builds lose to tanks
- Tanks lose to DoT (can't cleanse fast enough)
- DoT builds lose to burst (one-shot before DoT ramps)

### 4.3 Skill Scaling in PvP vs. PvE

Certain skills are intentionally adjusted for PvP:

**PvE Damage**: 1,000 per hit (enemy HP = 5,000)
**PvP Damage**: 300 per hit (player HP = 3,000, lower scaling)

This prevents one-shot builds from instantly winning. Skilled play with 50% hits becomes meta.

## Part 5: Advanced PvP Strategies

### 5.1 Positioning and Spacing

**Safe Distance**: maintain position outside opponent's action range
- Spell range: 20 units (stay >20 units for projectile spells)
- Attack range: 10 units (stay >10 units for melee)
- Movement speed differential: if you're 10% faster, you control spacing forever

**Arena Landmarks**: use terrain features
- Pillars: block line of sight (disrupt projectile builds)
- Ramps: gain height advantage (20% damage to downhill opponents)
- Corners: trap opponents (they can't escape, neither can you)

### 5.2 Mana/Resource Management

**Spell Builds**: track opponent's mana
- If mana pool = 200, opponent can cast 4× spell echo
- Force casting: waste mana on dodged spells
- Exhaust: opponent runs out of mana, can't cast

**Attack Builds**: track opponent's charges
- Frenzy charges: each provides +10% DPS
- Breaches: use abilities that consume charges (weaken opponent)
- Generate: kill adds to gain charges (if available)

### 5.3 Rotation Optimization

**Spell Builds** (optimal 6-second rotation):
1. Position safely (1 second)
2. Cast spell (0.5 seconds)
3. Cast spell (0.5 seconds)
4. Dodge expected counterattack (1 second)
5. Repeat

**Attack Builds** (optimal 6-second rotation):
1. Position adjacently (1 second)
2. Attack 3× (1.5 seconds)
3. Dodge (0.5 seconds)
4. Reposition (1 second)
5. Repeat

Executing the optimal rotation under pressure is the primary skill difference between good and great PvP players.

### 5.4 Emotional Management

PvP is high-stress. Professional players report:
- Mental fatigue from sustained concentration
- Tilt (emotional frustration) leading to mistakes
- Decision-making quality decreases under pressure

Techniques to mitigate:
- Practice specific scenarios 100+ times (reduce mental load)
- Breathwork between matches (reset emotional state)
- Record matches (review mistakes without tilt)

## Part 6: Tournament Meta and Pro Strategies

### 6.1 Tournament Rules and Bans

**Standard Tournament Ruleset**:
- Format: Swiss system (4 rounds, seed based on prior rounds)
- Each round: 1v1 best-of-3 matches (first to 2 wins)
- Ban phase: before series, 2 builds banned per player

**Most Banned Builds** (current season):
- Crit Spell Echo (30% ban rate)
- Block Paladin (25% ban rate)
- Poison Assassin (15% ban rate)

High ban rates indicate balance issues (developers nerf these builds).

### 6.2 Tournament Strategy

**Pick and Ban Phase**:
1. Opponent picks build
2. You pick counter-build (based on rock-paper-scissors matchup)
3. Opponent picks 2nd build (if available)
4. You pick 2nd build (if available)

Example:
- Opponent picks Spell Echo (burst)
- You pick Block Paladin (counter to burst)
- Opponent picks Poison Assassin (counter to paladin)
- You pick Crit Deadeye (counter to assassin)

### 6.3 Professional Player Strategies

**Top 10 Players' Preferences** (based on tournament records):
1. Flexibility (multiple viable builds, adapt to meta)
2. Mechanical skill (execution > build power)
3. Decision-making (read opponent, adapt)
4. Experience (1,000+ matches played)
5. Mentality (tilt resistance, composure)
