# Path of Exile 2: Player Progression and Meta Overview

## Part 1: Character Progression Milestones

### 1.1 Early Game Milestones (Acts 1-2)

**Level 10-20 Progression**:
- Quest 1: Kill Brutus (Act 1) → grants "Raise Zombie" gem
- Quest 2: Kill Hailrake (Act 1) → grants "Added Cold Damage" support
- Quest 3: Kill Merveil (Act 1 boss) → grants weapon/armor upgrade, acts 2 access
- Level check: should be 15-18 before starting act 2

**Common mistakes**:
- Over-leveling (killing optional adds): wastes time, minimal XP gain
- Under-gearing (skipping vendor upgrades): 10-20% slower progression
- Wrong skill gems: using early-game gems into act 2 (lose 50% DPS)

### 1.2 Mid Game Milestones (Acts 3-4)

**Level 30-45 Progression**:
- Quest 1: Clear Pyramid Labyrinth (Act 3) → access Ascendancy passive tree (40% power increase)
- Quest 2: Kill Piety (Act 3 boss) → grants major gear upgrade
- Quest 3: Kill Gravicius (Act 4) → grants first unique item often
- Quest 4: Kill Doedre (Act 4) → grants skill gem library unlock

**Ascendancy passive allocation strategy**:
- Pick 1 starting node (40% bonus in that domain)
- Path to 1 connector node (enables adjacent ascendancy nodes)
- Total: 4-5 passive point investment yields 40-50% power increase

### 1.3 Late Game Milestones (Acts 5-6)

**Level 50-70 Progression**:
- Quest 1: Kill Kitava Act 5 (final act boss) → lose resistances (-30%), gain map access
- Quest 2: Clear 10 white maps (tier 1-2) → farm for progression gear
- Quest 3: Kill Kitava Act 6 (absolute final boss) → unlock endgame, maps tier 1-16

**Mandatory resistance rebalancing**:
- After Kitava Act 5: lose 30% resist, must re-farm resistance gear immediately
- After Kitava Act 6: same resistance loss, now have access to rare maps for gear farming

## Part 2: Skill Gem Progression

### 2.1 Gem Quality and Leveling

Skill gems gain experience (scaled to 10% of character XP) and level up from 1-20 (base) to 1-21 (corrupted).

Each gem level grants:
- Damage scaling: +5-8% per level (compounding)
- Cooldown reduction: -2% per level
- Mana cost: +5% per level
- Attack/cast speed: +1% per level

**Total scaling from gem level 1→20**:
- Damage: +150% (3x multiplier)
- Cooldown: -40% (2.5x more frequent)
- Total DPS: 4.5x improvement from gem leveling alone

Prioritizing gem leveling (use high-level gems) yields more DPS than gear upgrades early-game.

### 2.2 Support Gem Synergies

Support gems add bonuses to linked active gems. Key combos:

**Physical Damage Stack**:
- Active: Earthquake, Cleave, Reave
- Support 1: Melee Physical Damage (+30% phys)
- Support 2: Multistrike (3 hits per cast)
- Support 3: Added Fire Damage (+elemental scaling)
- Total: 3.0 × 1.3 (phys) = 3.9x base DPS

**Spell Stack**:
- Active: Fireball, Ice Spear, Spell Echo
- Support 1: Spell Echo (repeat spell, +60% DPS)
- Support 2: Added Cold Damage (elemental scaling)
- Support 3: Increased Area of Effect (+range)
- Total: 1.6x DPS + 50% area increase (covers 2.25x space)

### 2.3 Gem Optimization Strategy

**Early game (acts 1-3)**:
- Focus: damage gems only (Melee Phys, Added Fire, Multistrike)
- Ignore: utility gems (don't have space)
- Result: 5-socket body armor = 3 active + 2 support gems

**Mid game (acts 4-5)**:
- Add: utility gems (Vaal skills for burst damage)
- Focus: balanced damage + survivability
- Result: 6-socket body armor = 3 active + 3 support gems

**Endgame (maps tier 1-16)**:
- Optimize: every slot for maximum damage synergy
- Add: conditional gems for specific bosses
- Result: 6 sockets + 4 off-hand links = 10+ active gems total

## Part 3: Passive Tree Strategy

### 3.1 Path Efficiency and Scaling

Passive tree efficiency is measured in nodes-per-point:

**Efficient path**: 
- Start class node (15% scaling)
- Path to next node (3 points × +10% damage = 30% total)
- Allocate in single direction

**Cost-to-benefit ratio**:
- 15% → 3 points = 5% per point (baseline)
- 30% → 5 points = 6% per point (good path)
- 50% → 8 points = 6.25% per point (excellent path)

Strategic pathing avoids waste: don't path through low-value nodes for distant high-value nodes.

### 3.2 Keystones and Build Identity

Keystones fundamentally change playstyles:

**Unbreakable** (1,200 poise + stagger immunity):
- Enables: immobile tanky playstyle (Cyclone tanks)
- Enables: face-tank dangerous bosses without kiting
- Cost: 1 keystone (high value)

**Acrobatics** (+30% dodge, -50% armor):
- Enables: evasion-only playstyle (no armor investment)
- Enables: crit scaling (dodge synergizes with crit)
- Cost: 1 keystone + -50% armor (moderate value)

**Chaos Inoculation** (life = 1 HP, chaos immunity):
- Enables: pure chaos immunity (ignore all chaos damage)
- Enables: CI stacking with mana (hybrid build)
- Cost: 1 keystone + 1 HP (high risk, high reward)

### 3.3 Tree Pathing for Specific Builds

**Earthquake Berserker Path**:
1. Start: Cleaving (physical nodes)
2. Path: Elasticity (armor cluster)
3. Allocate: Martial Training (attack nodes)
4. End: Duelist Ascendancy entry (more attack nodes)
Total: 80 points to standard berserker setup

**Incinerate Templar Path**:
1. Start: Magister (spell nodes)
2. Path: Spell Amplification (fire cluster)
3. Allocate: Cast Speed nodes
4. End: Spell Damage keystone (if available in endgame)
Total: 80 points to standard templar setup

## Part 4: Meta and Current Viability

### 4.1 Tier 1 Meta Builds (PoE2 Current)

**Physical Damage Builds** (60% popularity):
- Earthquake Berserker: proven, simple, high DPS
- Cyclone Warrior: high survivability, mobile
- Cleave Gladiator: bleed scaling enabler

**Spell Builds** (30% popularity):
- Incinerate Templar: fire DoT scaling, ignite synergy
- Spell Echo builds: multi-projectile scaling
- Archmage Hierophant: mana-scaling builds

**Hybrid Builds** (10% popularity):
- CI + Mana Templar: chaos immune + defensive
- Poison Assassin: physical-to-chaos conversion
- Dual-scaling Sorcerer: elemental + physical hybrid

### 4.2 Meta Shifts Between League Starts and Mid-League

**League Start (First Week)**:
- Simplicity: favor straightforward builds (Earthquake > complex conversions)
- Speed: fast maps > endgame bossing builds
- Accessibility: rare-geared (no unique requirements)

**Mid-League (Weeks 3-8)**:
- Specialization: unique-enabled builds (Acuity crit, CI immunity)
- Complex scaling: conversion/hybrid builds viable with investment
- Bossing: dedicated boss-killer builds emerge

**Late-League (Week 8+)**:
- Optimization: pixel-perfect gearing, legacy builds
- Economy: prices stabilize, crafting becomes meta
- Challenge: ultra-hard builds pushed (sub-5-minute tier 16 farming)

### 4.3 Build Viability Tiers

**Tier S (Always Viable)**: 
- Earthquake: consistent damage, simple scaling
- Cyclone: consistent clear speed, tank playstyle
- Incinerate: fire DoT, endgame ignite scaling

**Tier A (Viable with Investment)**:
- Poison Assassin: requires gem scaling but pays off endgame
- Bleed Glad: physical stacking, Crimson Dance scaling
- Spell Echo: requires unique enablers but extreme damage

**Tier B (Niche/Challenge)**:
- Pure chaos builds: countered by chaos-immune rares
- Bleed-only: countered by bleed-immune map mods
- Crit-conversion: overly dependent on critical multiplier stacking

**Tier C (Not Viable)**:
- Early-game skills used endgame (Fireball vs. Incinerate damage gap: 4x)
- Builds ignoring core mechanics (no leech on leech-required fights)
- Builds without ascendancy allocation (40% power loss)

## Part 5: Newbie-to-Endgame Progression Path

### 5.1 Recommended Sequence for First Playthrough

**Phase 1: Acts 1-3 (Gentle Introduction)**
- Pick simple skill (Earthquake or Fireball)
- Don't optimize, just kill bosses
- Learn boss mechanics, dodge, timing
- Result: reach act 3, level 30, basic understanding

**Phase 2: Acts 4-5 (Gear Awareness)**
- Start crafting rares (transmutation + alteration path)
- Identify mandatory resistances (75% cap)
- Learn optimal damage+survivability balance
- Result: reach act 5, level 45, understand economy

**Phase 3: Acts 6 (Endgame Gateway)**
- Allocate Ascendancy passives (2-3 points)
- Transition to rare farming (white maps)
- First gear trade with other players
- Result: reach act 6 completion, level 65, ready for endgame

**Phase 4: Maps 1-5 (Rapid Progression)**
- Farm white maps (tier 1-5)
- Sell drops, reinvest in better gear
- Build wealth: 100 → 500 chaos
- Result: reach tier 6 maps, level 75, solid gear

**Phase 5: Maps 6-10 (Mid-Endgame)**
- Start target farming unique drops
- Craft better rares (exalted-level investment)
- Accumulate 1,000+ chaos wealth
- Result: reach tier 11 maps, level 80, endgame-capable

**Phase 6: Maps 11-16 (Endgame Mastery)**
- Optimize every piece of gear
- Implement sextant/scarab strategy
- Target 500-1,000 chaos/hour farm rate
- Result: reach tier 16 completion, level 85+, true endgame

Total time investment: 40-60 hours for full progression.

### 5.2 Resource Management Principles

**Early Game**:
- Keep 50 chaos minimum (safety buffer for gear)
- Spend 10-20 chaos per item (low investment)
- Sell all rares (learn what's valuable later)

**Mid Game**:
- Keep 200 chaos minimum (gear upgrade buffer)
- Spend 50-100 chaos per item (medium investment)
- Vendor rares by rarity (identify high-value bases)

**Endgame**:
- Keep 1,000 chaos minimum (sextant/scarab funding)
- Spend 100-500 chaos per item (high investment)
- Trade selectively (6-socket bodies worth 500+, perfect rolls matter)

**Meta principle**: Never spend more than 20% of liquid wealth on single item.

## Conclusion

Player progression in PoE2 is non-linear. Optimal strategy emphasizes:
1. Simple builds with high scaling ceilings
2. Early gear optimization (resistance caps, life targets)
3. Passive tree pathing for specific archetype
4. Gem selection for multiplicative damage scaling
5. Wealth accumulation through farming
6. Reinvestment in incremental gear upgrades

Following this progression, players reach endgame content and true "mastery" within 40-60 hours, enabling deep engagement with game systems.
