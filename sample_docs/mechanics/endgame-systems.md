# Path of Exile 2: Endgame Systems Reference

## Overview

Path of Exile 2 Endgame begins after completing Act 6. The endgame consists of several interconnected systems that provide content at increasing difficulty tiers.

---

## 1. Atlas of Worlds

### What is the Atlas
- The Atlas of Worlds is the endgame map progression system.
- Each node on the Atlas represents a map tier and biome.
- Maps are run by placing them in the Map Device and activating them.

### Map Tiers
- Tier 1-5: White maps; entry-level endgame.
- Tier 6-10: Yellow maps; intermediate endgame.
- Tier 11-16: Red maps; advanced endgame.

### Map Modifiers
- Maps can have prefixes and suffixes that alter conditions.
- Prefix examples: "Monsters deal 25% more elemental damage", "Map has additional rare monsters".
- Suffix examples: "Players cannot regenerate life, mana, or energy shield", "Monsters have 100% increased movement speed".

### Atlas Passive Tree
- Separate passive tree for Atlas that amplifies map properties.
- 120 passive points available; earned by completing maps.
- Notable clusters: Wandering Path (30% more maps found), Shaping the Atlas, Singular Focus.

## 2. Endgame Boss Encounters

### Pinnacle Bosses
- Pinnacle bosses require specific materials to access.
- Drop conditions reset weekly.
- Access materials drop from Atlas map bosses.

### Maven
- Requires defeating all 10 Atlas bosses in one session while The Maven watches.
- The Maven then offers an encounter with herself.
- Phase 1: memory game where player must navigate glowing orbs without touching them.
- Phase 2: standard boss with witness ability that speeds up the fight.
- Verification Fact: Maven's memory game phase requires navigating exactly 10 orbs in sequence.

### The Feared
- Requires: Invitations from each of 5 specific bosses.
- Fought simultaneously in a single encounter.
- Strategy: kite clockwise, focus one boss at a time, never engage two simultaneously.
- Verification Fact: The Feared consists of exactly 5 simultaneous boss encounters.

### Arbiter of Ash (Endgame)
- Highest baseline difficulty in dataset.
- Stagger threshold: 310 per phase.
- Three phases with full mechanic shifts between.
- Drop rate for unique items: 2%.
- Verification Fact: Arbiter of Ash stagger threshold is 310, higher than all Act bosses.

## 3. Delve System

### Overview
- Delve is an infinite depth cave-mining system.
- Player uses a Crawler (mining cart) as a light source.
- Darkness outside the Crawler light radius deals rapidly scaling damage.

### Sulphite
- Sulphite is the fuel for the Crawler.
- Collected from maps by clicking glowing Sulphite deposits.
- Capacity starts at 300 and can be upgraded via Niko's shop.

### Depth Scaling
- Boss difficulty, rewards, and darkness damage scale with depth.
- At depth 100: moderate challenge.
- At depth 300: very high difficulty.
- At depth 600+: extreme challenge; builds must be specifically optimized.

### Biomes and Notable Nodes
- Biomes appear at random and contain specific fossils and resonators.
- Notable biome: The Azurite Mine (all depths) - primary fossil source.
- Fossils modify crafted items in specific elemental or stat directions.

---

## 4. Heist System

### Overview
- Heist is a stealth-adjacent content system where players complete jobs with Rogue NPC partners.
- Jobs involve retrieving a target item from a guarded location.
- Each action increases the Alert Level; if Alert reaches maximum, guards breach the vault.

### Alert Level Management
- Opening doors increases Alert by 3-8 per door.
- Killing guards increases Alert by 5-10 per guard.
- Interacting with the Loot Room increases Alert by 15.
- Maximum Alert Level before breach: 100.

### Grand Heists (Blueprints)
- Grand Heists are multi-room versions.
- Up to 4 wings; each wing requires a different Rogue partner.
- Unique rewards only available from Grand Heists: Alternate Quality Gems, Replica Unique Items.

### Rogue Partners
- Each Rogue has a specialty (Lockpicking, Agility, Engineering, Perception, etc.).
- Rogue level (1-5) determines success rate for door unlocking.
- Higher level Rogues require more Gold upfront.

## 5. Blight System

### Overview
- Blight encounters are tower defense events on top of regular map content.
- A Blight pump in the center must be protected from waves of monsters.
- Placing towers along monster lanes blocks and damages enemies.

### Tower Types
- Obstructing Tower: slows enemy movement by 30%.
- Chilling Tower: chills enemies, reducing their speed.
- Empowering Tower: buffs nearby towers by 25%.
- Freezing Tower: periodically freezes groups.
- Smothering Tower: reduces enemy resistances by 20%.
- Temporal Tower: applies temporal chains curse.

### Blighted Maps
- Special maps where the entire map is a Blight encounter.
- Higher rewards; dramatically more enemies.
- Requires tower-heavy strategy from the start.

---

## 6. Incursion System

### Overview
- Incursion sends the player back in time to a temple being built.
- The player has a limited time to kill enemies and upgrade rooms.
- After 11 Incursions, the completed Atzoatl temple becomes accessible.

### Room Upgrading
- Rooms start at tier 1.
- Upgrading requires killing the architect of that room.
- Tier 3 rooms have the best rewards: Sacrifice room (Divine Orbs), Vault (high currency).

### Temple Strategy
- Pre-plan which rooms to upgrade based on your build needs.
- Connected rooms provide bonus effects.
- Nexus room connects to all adjacent rooms; prioritize upgrading it.

## 7. Endgame Currency Economy

### Orb Hierarchy (Value Tier, Highest to Lowest)
1. Mirror of Kalandra: mirrors any non-unique item.
2. Chaos Orb: re-rolls all modifiers on a rare item.
3. Divine Orb: re-rolls values within existing modifier ranges.
4. Exalted Orb: adds one random modifier to a rare item.
5. Orb of Annulment: removes one random modifier from a rare item.
6. Veiled Chaos Orb: rerolls and unveils one modifier.
7. Orb of Alteration: re-rolls all modifiers on a magic item.
8. Orb of Transmutation: upgrades a normal item to magic.
9. Orb of Augmentation: adds a modifier to a magic item.
10. Scroll of Wisdom: identifies an unidentified item.

### Trade and Economy Notes
- Prices are denominated in Chaos Orbs by convention.
- Mirror of Kalandra average exchange rate: approximately 200,000 Chaos Orbs.
- Divine Orb exchange rate: approximately 200-250 Chaos Orbs.
- Exalted Orb exchange rate: approximately 150-180 Chaos Orbs.

## 8. Map Sustain Strategy

### White Map Phase (Tier 1-5)
- Run all white maps you find to unlock yellow tier.
- Do not brick white maps with high monster damage mods if your build is new.

### Yellow Map Phase (Tier 6-10)
- Prioritize maps in connected clusters to unlock red maps faster.
- Sell maps you cannot run due to mods.

### Red Map Phase (Tier 11-16)
- Focus on completing Awakening Bonus objectives for Atlas passive points.
- Run preferred map biomes for targeted boss drop farming.

### Sustain Formula
- To sustain Tier 16 maps, need 3+ maps per map run on average.
- Increased item quantity on character: target 100%+ IIQ for red maps.
- Atlas passives that increase map drop quantity are mandatory for sustain.
