# Path of Exile 2: Rare Affix Priority Guide

## Part 1: Priority Principle

When a rare monster has multiple affixes, do not read them as equal. Some affixes create immediate danger while others merely make the pack longer to kill. The practical order is always the same: damage spike first, speed second, utility third, tankiness last.

## Part 2: Damage Spike Affixes

### 2.1 Added Damage

Added damage is the most common burst amplifier.
- Added fire damage increases danger against low fire resistance.
- Added cold damage increases danger if freeze or chill also appears.
- Added lightning damage increases danger when shock can stack.
- Added chaos damage is dangerous because chaos resistance is often lower than elemental resistance.

If a rare has added damage plus any speed buff, treat it as a high-priority target.

### 2.2 Crit and Multiplier Style Affixes

Some rares scale their danger through crit-like behavior or direct multiplier effects.
- They may hit much harder than a normal rare.
- They may create sudden one-shot patterns.
- They usually become more dangerous when paired with movement speed.

The key rule: if a monster can burst you before you can recover, kill it first.

## Part 3: Speed Affixes

### 3.1 Movement Speed

Movement speed changes how a rare reaches you.
- Faster rares reduce reaction time.
- Faster rares force longer chase patterns.
- Faster rares are more dangerous in narrow maps.

### 3.2 Attack or Cast Speed

Attack and cast speed change how often the rare can threaten you.
- Fast attackers can chain hits before you reset position.
- Fast casters can fill the arena with hazards.
- Fast rares often become lethal when combined with on-hit effects.

### 3.3 Chase Rule

If a rare is both fast and damaging, do not try to tank it and trade hits.
Back away, isolate it, and remove the threat source before resuming clear.

## Part 4: Utility Affixes

### 4.1 Summoning

Summoning affixes are dangerous because they increase clutter.
- Adds can body-block movement.
- Adds can refresh boss-like pressure.
- Adds can hide the rare in a crowd.

Kill the summoner if the adds are creating pressure.

### 4.2 Ground Effects

Ground effects punish standing still.
- Poison pools.
- Burning zones.
- Shocked ground.
- Slow fields.

If the rare keeps the floor unsafe, the fight becomes a positioning test rather than a damage race.

### 4.3 Curse-Like Mods

Curse-like mods are often underestimated.
- They may lower your damage.
- They may lower your movement speed.
- They may lower your recovery or defenses.

These are usually not as deadly as burst damage, but they make every other problem worse.

## Part 5: Defense Affixes

### 5.1 Extra Life

Extra life is not always a priority danger by itself.
- It increases time-to-kill.
- It becomes dangerous when paired with speed or hazards.
- It is especially annoying when the pack is already mobile.

### 5.2 Armor or Resistance

Defense affixes lengthen the fight.
- Longer fights mean more chances for mistakes.
- More chances for mistakes mean more deaths.
- More deaths mean more time lost.

Still, these affixes usually sit below raw damage and speed in priority.

## Part 6: Combined Affix Ranking

### 6.1 Highest Priority

These combinations deserve instant attention:
- Damage + speed.
- Damage + projectiles.
- Damage + on-death explosions.
- Damage + ground hazards.
- Speed + summoning.

### 6.2 Medium Priority

These combinations are dangerous but manageable:
- Tankiness + hazards.
- Summoning + curse effects.
- Defense + speed.
- Minor damage + minor speed.

### 6.3 Lower Priority

These are still worth noting but are not the first target:
- Pure tankiness.
- Pure utility without damage.
- Minor extra life with no speed.
- Low-impact resistance boosts.

## Part 7: Combat Target Rules

### 7.1 One-Rare Pack

If the pack contains only one rare, focus on whether it can kill you before you finish the kill.
- If yes, play safe.
- If no, finish it quickly.

### 7.2 Multi-Rare Pack

If the pack contains several rares:
1. Identify the one with speed.
2. Identify the one with burst damage.
3. Remove the one that creates the most chaos.
4. Leave the tankiest rare for last.

### 7.3 Boss-Rare Hybrid

Some content creates rare-like enemies inside a boss fight.
When that happens, the rules become stricter:
- Kill anything that threatens movement.
- Keep the arena clear.
- Do not tunnel the main target if the supporting enemies are about to create a lethal window.

## Part 8: Practical Shortcut

If you are too busy to parse all affixes in real time, use a shortcut:
- Red or fast-looking monster first.
- Explosive or summoning monster second.
- Tanky monster last.

This is not perfect, but it is usually good enough to prevent deaths.

## Conclusion

Rare affix priority is a survival skill. The player who kills the right monster first clears faster, spends fewer flasks, and dies less often. The player who ignores priority often dies to the least obvious threat in the pack.
