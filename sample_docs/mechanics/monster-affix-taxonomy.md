# Path of Exile 2: Monster Affix Taxonomy

## Part 1: Monster Classes

### 1.1 Normal Monsters

Normal monsters are the baseline unit of danger.

**Traits**:
- Lowest health.
- Lowest damage.
- No special modifiers.
- Frequently appear in packs.

**Use in gameplay**:
- Generate flask charges.
- Fill map density.
- Create baseline risk for clear speed builds.

### 1.2 Magic Monsters

Magic monsters have one or two affixes that make them stronger than normal monsters.

**Traits**:
- Blue nameplate.
- One or two special modifiers.
- Slightly higher health and damage.
- Often serve as mini-checkpoints in maps.

**Examples**:
- Increased movement speed.
- Extra elemental damage.
- Extra minions.

### 1.3 Rare Monsters

Rare monsters are the key danger spike in normal mapping.

**Traits**:
- Yellow nameplate.
- Multiple affixes.
- Much higher health than normal monsters.
- Can create lethal combinations when affixes stack badly.

**Why they matter**:
- They can force flask use.
- They can stall clear speed.
- They can kill undergeared builds instantly if ignored.

### 1.4 Unique Monsters

Unique monsters have fixed identity and usually fixed mechanics.

**Traits**:
- Named boss-like enemies.
- Often tied to quests or maps.
- Usually have custom skill sets.
- Can be easier or harder than rare monsters depending on the fight.

### 1.5 Boss Monsters

Boss monsters are the highest-threat classification in normal content.

**Traits**:
- Scripted attacks.
- Phase transitions.
- High damage windows.
- Arena mechanics.

Bosses are not just stronger rares. They are encounter design units with rules.

## Part 2: Affix Families

### 2.1 Damage Affixes

**Common damage affixes**:
- Increased physical damage.
- Added fire damage.
- Added cold damage.
- Added lightning damage.
- Extra chaos damage.

**Effect**:
- Increase raw threat.
- Scale very hard against low-defense builds.
- Often combine with speed affixes to create burst kills.

### 2.2 Speed Affixes

**Common speed affixes**:
- Increased movement speed.
- Increased attack speed.
- Increased cast speed.
- Faster recovery between actions.

**Effect**:
- Reduce reaction time.
- Make enemy patterns harder to read.
- Turn manageable packs into lethal ones.

### 2.3 Defense Affixes

**Common defense affixes**:
- Extra life.
- Extra armor.
- Extra evasion.
- Extra elemental resistance.
- Extra chaos resistance.

**Effect**:
- Increase time-to-kill.
- Extend dangerous mechanics.
- Make map bosses and rares harder to burst.

### 2.4 Utility Affixes

**Common utility affixes**:
- Summon allies.
- Create ground hazards.
- Apply curse-like debuffs.
- Reflect damage.
- Spawn explosions on death.

**Effect**:
- Increase complexity.
- Force movement.
- Create layered threat profiles.

## Part 3: Rare Monster Affix Combinations

### 3.1 Dangerous Pairings

Some affix combinations are much more dangerous than others.

**High danger combinations**:
- Extra damage + movement speed.
- Extra damage + attack speed.
- Ground hazard + extra life.
- Projectile spam + extra damage.
- On-death explosion + fast movement.

### 3.2 Moderate Pairings

These combinations are dangerous but usually manageable.

**Examples**:
- Extra life + extra armor.
- Extra movement speed + minor elemental damage.
- Summons + extra defense.
- Curse effect + moderate damage.

### 3.3 Low Threat Pairings

These are annoying but not usually lethal.

**Examples**:
- Minor accuracy bonus.
- Small mana regeneration.
- Low impact elemental resistance.
- Cosmetic or flavor modifiers.

## Part 4: Map Modifiers and Monster Scaling

### 4.1 Map Modifiers

Map modifiers change the entire monster population of a map.

**Examples**:
- Monsters deal more damage.
- Monsters have more life.
- Monsters move faster.
- Monsters cast or attack faster.
- Monsters spawn in higher numbers.

### 4.2 Scaling Rules

When a map modifier increases monster threat, the effect is multiplicative with rare affixes and boss mechanics.

**Example**:
- Base rare monster damage: 100.
- Map modifier: +50% damage.
- Rare affix: +25% damage.
- Final damage: 100 × 1.5 × 1.25 = 187.5.

This is why even seemingly small modifiers can become dangerous when stacked.

### 4.3 Player Response

**Safe response**:
- Skip dangerous map if your build is weak.
- Roll another map.
- Keep currency rolling instead of forcing bad content.

**Aggressive response**:
- Use flask setup and strong positioning.
- Bait dangerous monsters out of packs.
- Kill the most dangerous rare first.

## Part 5: Monster Threat Prioritization

### 5.1 Priority Order

When a pack contains multiple threats, kill in this order:
1. Monster with the strongest damage spike.
2. Monster with the most dangerous speed buff.
3. Monster that creates area hazards.
4. Monster that summons additional enemies.
5. Monster that is merely tanky.

### 5.2 Why Prioritization Works

The most dangerous monster is rarely the tankiest one. It is the one that can create a kill condition before you finish clearing the pack.

### 5.3 Example Pack

A rare pack might include:
- One rare with extra damage and speed.
- Two magic monsters with summon modifiers.
- Ten normal monsters.

The correct choice is to kill the rare first, then the summoners, then the trash.

## Part 6: Hardcore vs Softcore Threat Models

### 6.1 Hardcore

In hardcore, any rare monster can be deadly if the affix stack is bad enough.

**Principles**:
- Do not assume visibility equals safety.
- Do not stay in melee range if the pack is unscouted.
- Treat every strong rare as a real boss check.

### 6.2 Softcore

In softcore, the same rare might only cost time and a portal.

**Principles**:
- You can take more risks.
- You can learn more through death.
- You still lose efficiency if you ignore threat patterns.

### 6.3 Shared Rule

Regardless of mode, speed affixes and damage affixes deserve immediate attention. If a rare moves fast and hits hard, it is a priority kill.

---

## Conclusion

Monster affix taxonomy helps players identify which enemies deserve attention. The goal is not to memorize every combination. The goal is to know which traits turn a normal pack into a lethal encounter.

When in doubt, kill the source of burst damage first, then the source of speed, then the source of utility.
