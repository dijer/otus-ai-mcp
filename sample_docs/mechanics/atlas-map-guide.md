# Path of Exile 2: Map Mechanics and Atlas Strategy Guide

## Overview

This guide covers everything needed to efficiently progress through the endgame Atlas of Worlds, including map mechanics, atlas passive strategy, and farming efficiency.

---

## Part 1: Map Basics

### What is a Map

A map is a consumable item that creates an isolated area for the player to fight through. Maps are found as loot from other maps and from boss drops. They contain a unique layout, a specific biome, and a boss encounter at the end.

### Map Properties

Each map has:
- **Tier**: Determines difficulty and item level of drops (Tier 1 to Tier 16).
- **Biome**: Visual theme and monster type (Desert, Forest, Cave, Arctic, etc.).
- **Base Modifiers**: Default conditions inherent to that map's layout.
- **Player-Applied Modifiers**: Orbs of Scouring, Chaos Orbs, and Vaal Orbs can modify maps.

### Map Item Level

- Map item level = 67 + Map Tier.
- Tier 1 maps: item level 68.
- Tier 16 maps: item level 83.
- Most high-end gear requires item level 82-83 as base.

## Part 2: Map Modifiers in Detail

### Prefix Modifiers (Buffs for Enemies)

**Area Contains Increased Rare Monsters**
- +1 to +4 additional rare monsters.
- Increases item quantity significantly.
- Recommended: keep this mod on all maps.

**Monsters Deal X% More Elemental Damage**
- Ranges from 10% to 50% more.
- Dangerous for builds without high elemental resistances.
- Avoid above 30% if resistance is not capped at 75%.

**Monsters Have X% Increased Maximum Life**
- Ranges from 25% to 100%.
- Slows map clear speed.
- Acceptable on most builds; avoid 100% if time-efficient farming matters.

**Players Cannot Leech Life, Mana, or Energy Shield**
- Hard counter for leech-based builds.
- Safe to run with flask-based recovery.
- High risk for Vaal Pact or leech-dependent builds.

**Monsters Reflect X% Physical Damage**
- Ranges from 8% to 18%.
- Very dangerous for physical attack builds.
- Safe for spell builds or builds with "cannot reflect" items.

**Monsters Reflect X% Elemental Damage**
- Ranges from 8% to 18%.
- Dangerous for elemental attack builds.
- Never run both physical and elemental reflect on the same map.

**Area is Inhabited by 2 Additional Rogue Exiles**
- Rogue Exiles are powerful human-form enemies.
- High item value; they drop rare items.
- Low risk; acceptable on all builds.

### Suffix Modifiers (Debuffs for Players)

**Players Cannot Regenerate Life, Mana, or Energy Shield**
- Eliminates all regeneration.
- Run with flask sustain as primary recovery.
- Hard counter for CI (Chaos Inoculation) builds that rely on ES regen.

**Players Have X% Reduced Effect of Auras**
- Reduces Grace, Determination, Haste, etc.
- Dangerous for builds that rely heavily on one aura.
- Run if your build has redundant defenses.

**Monsters Have X% Chance to Avoid Elemental Ailments**
- Reduces shock, freeze, ignite reliability.
- Dangerous for ailment-dependent builds like Ignite Elementalist.
- Safe for builds that do not rely on ailment application.

**Area Has X% Less Monster Density**
- Reduces map clear speed.
- Low risk to survivability.
- Avoid if farming for map drops (lower density = fewer maps found).

**Players Are Cursed with Temporal Chains**
- Slows all player actions by 20%.
- Significantly reduces dodge effectiveness.
- Safe with Temporal Chains immunity on flask or ring.

**Players Are Cursed with Enfeeble**
- Reduces damage dealt by 15-20%.
- Lowest risk debuff modifier.
- Acceptable on all builds.

## Part 3: Atlas Passive Tree Strategy

### Early Atlas (First 30 Points)

Recommended nodes in priority order:
1. Natural Affinity: 8% more maps found in your area.
2. Wandering Path: all atlas passive bonuses doubled, but only if no keystones allocated.
3. Master Cartographer: reduces cost of upgrading maps.
4. Singular Focus: reduces biome variety, increasing chance of preferred maps.
5. One Step Ahead: maps are more likely to have 4 prefix modifiers when sold.

### Mid Atlas (Points 30-80)

Focus based on preferred farming strategy:

**For Boss Farming:**
- Boss difficulty nodes (increased boss life = increased item drops).
- Guardian Map nodes.
- Invitation frequency nodes.

**For General Mapping:**
- Monster pack size nodes.
- Increased rare monster nodes.
- Scarab quantity nodes.

**For Specific Content:**
- Delve: Sulphite Scarcity node.
- Heist: Heist Job Frequency node.
- Blight: Tower Effectiveness nodes.

### Late Atlas (Points 80-120)

Complete remaining preferred content nodes. Respec nodes that no longer serve the current strategy using Orbs of Unmaking.

## Part 4: Map Sustain at Tier 16

### The Sustain Formula

To indefinitely run Tier 16 maps, you need:
- Average 3.0+ maps per map run.
- At 100% increased item quantity, expect approximately 2.5-3.5 maps per run.
- At 150%+ IIQ, expect 4.0+ maps per run.

### How to Increase Map Drops

1. **Increased Item Quantity on Gear**: every 10% IIQ adds approximately 0.3 maps per run.
2. **Quantity of Maps Found Atlas Passives**: stacking map-find passives multiplies base drops.
3. **Map Modifiers**: run maps with more rare/unique monsters for higher base drops.
4. **Cartographer's Delight Scarab**: guaranteed additional map drop.
5. **Running Maps in Sextant-Blocked Zones**: Sextant modifiers can add monster count.

## Part 5: Sextant System

### What are Sextants

Sextants are currency items that apply modifiers to Atlas regions for 3 map uses.

### Sextant Types

**Apprentice Sextant**
- Applied to White Atlas regions (Tier 1-5).
- Adds minor bonus modifiers.
- Low cost.

**Journeyman Sextant**
- Applied to Yellow Atlas regions (Tier 6-10).
- Moderate bonuses.
- Medium cost.

**Master Sextant**
- Applied to Red Atlas regions (Tier 11-16).
- Major bonuses; used for target farming.
- High cost; purchased from Zana or found in maps.

### Valuable Sextant Modifiers

**"Area contains an additional Delirium Mirror"**
- High-value; Delirium mirrors significantly increase item quantity.
- Trade value: Very high; sought in bulk.

**"Area contains an additional Expedition Encounter"**
- Good for Logbook farming.

**"Unique monsters drop an additional connected Map"**
- Excellent for map sustain.
- Verification Fact: This sextant modifier guarantees one additional map from unique monster kills.

**"Area is Haunted by X Spirits"**
- High monster density bonus.
- Excellent for general currency farming.

## Part 6: Scarab System

### What are Scarabs

Scarabs are items placed in the Map Device alongside a map to add specific content to the run.

### Scarab Tiers
- Rusted Scarab: minor bonus.
- Polished Scarab: moderate bonus.
- Gilded Scarab: major bonus.
- Winged Scarab: maximum bonus (rarest).

### Notable Scarab Types

**Cartographer's Scarab**
- Rusted: map boss drops an additional rare map.
- Polished: boss drops a map and one additional rare item.
- Gilded: boss drops 3 rare maps.
- Winged: boss drops 5 maps, one of which is 1 tier higher.

**Reliquary Scarab**
- Adds a chance for unique items to be from the Reliquary pool.
- Winged variant: 3 additional Reliquary items.

**Divination Scarab**
- Adds divination card drops to the area.
- Winged: doubles divination card drop rate.

**Legion Scarab**
- Adds a Legion encounter to the map.
- Gilded and Winged variants significantly increase Splinter drops.

**Bestiary Scarab**
- Adds Einhar to the map for Beastcrafting.
- Winged: Einhar appears twice.

## Part 7: Favorite Map Strategy

### Choosing a Favorite Map

The Atlas allows 3 maps to be designated as "Favorite Maps."
- Favorite maps appear 5x more often in that region.
- Choose maps with:
  1. Compact layout (faster clear time).
  2. Single-level (no vertical traversal).
  3. High boss loot potential.

### Top Tier Favorite Map Candidates

**Strand (Tier 11)**
- Linear layout, extremely fast clear.
- Boss: simple pattern, reliable punish windows.
- Best for: quantity farming.

**Jungle Valley (Tier 12)**
- Moderate density, compact circular layout.
- Boss: moderate difficulty.
- Best for: general mapping.

**Promenade (Tier 14)**
- High density with multiple packed corridors.
- Boss: two phases, good drop table.
- Best for: density farming.

**Crimson Temple (Tier 15)**
- Valuable unique drops; boss has specific high-value loot table.
- Boss: Purifier, moderate difficulty.
- Best for: targeted unique farming.

**Shaper's Realm (Tier 16)**
- Access to The Shaper.
- Not suitable for sustain farming; used for boss target farming only.

## Part 8: Atlas Completion Bonuses

### Completion Bonus Types

**Awakening Bonus**
- Completing a map for the first time grants permanent +1 Awakening level contribution.
- Full Atlas Awakening Level 8: significant bonus to endgame content availability.

**Shaper Guardians Bonus**
- Each Shaper Guardian map completed adds a permanent Atlas mod.
- All 4 Shaper Guardians: unlocks Shaper fight access.

**Elder Guardians Bonus**
- Each Elder Guardian completed adds Elder influence availability.
- All 4 Elder Guardians: unlocks Elder spawning system.

## Verification Facts
- Tier 16 maps have item level exactly 83.
- "Unique monsters drop an additional connected map" sextant guarantees exactly one additional map.
- Winged Cartographer's Scarab drops 5 maps with one being exactly 1 tier higher.
- Wandering Path atlas passive doubles all other atlas passive bonuses but requires zero keystones.
- Full Atlas Awakening Level is exactly level 8.
