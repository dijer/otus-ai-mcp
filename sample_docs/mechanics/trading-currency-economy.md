# Path of Exile 2: Advanced Trading Systems and Currency Mastery

## Part 1: Trading Mechanics

### 1.1 Player-to-Player Trading

**Trade Types**:
1. **Item Trading** (most common)
   - Player A wants: Kaom's Heart (unique armor)
   - Player B offers: 500 chaos orbs
   - Trade mechanics: meet in town, exchange items
   - Time: 2-3 minutes per trade

2. **Currency Trading**
   - Player A wants: 1,000 chaos orbs
   - Player B offers: 6 exalted orbs (150 chaos each = 900 total)
   - Rate conversion: dynamic (based on league economy)
   - Frequency: 100+ per hour (most traded)

3. **Group Trading** (party-based)
   - Multiple players in group pool currency
   - Distribute equipment to optimal members
   - Split unique drops by agreement
   - Time: 30 minutes per group equipping session

### 1.2 Trade Restrictions

**Account Binding**:
- Some items are "account bound" (can't trade)
- Quest reward items: account bound
- Corrupted items: sometimes account bound
- Character-specific items: "This item can only be equipped on [Character Name]"

**Trading Cooldowns**:
- Cooldown between trades: none (trade anytime)
- Frequency limit: none (spam trading allowed)
- Currency transfer: instant

### 1.3 Trade Safety

**Scam Prevention**:
- Items shown before confirming trade
- Both players must agree to trade
- Trade is reversible within 60 seconds if mistake

**Price Checking**:
- Use market price history (public data)
- Compare recent sales (last 7 days)
- Negotiate fairly (within 5-10% of market)

## Part 2: Currency and Its Functions

### 2.1 Primary Currency Tiers

**Tier 1: Transmutation Orbs** (base currency)
- Use: transmute white item to blue
- Drop rate: extremely common (1 per 10 monsters)
- Value: 0.04 chaos each
- Stack size: 20 per inventory slot
- Annual earning: thousands of orbs

**Tier 2: Alteration Orbs** (modifier rerolls)
- Use: reroll blue item modifiers
- Drop rate: common (1 per 30 monsters)
- Value: 0.02 chaos each
- Stack size: 20
- Primary use: crafting preparation

**Tier 3: Regal Orbs** (rare upgrades)
- Use: upgrade blue item to rare (lock mods)
- Drop rate: uncommon (1 per 200 monsters)
- Value: 5-8 chaos each
- Primary use: final crafting step

**Tier 4: Exalted Orbs** (modifier addition)
- Use: add new mod to rare item
- Drop rate: rare (1 per 5,000 monsters)
- Value: 150 chaos each
- Primary use: endgame crafting

**Tier 5: Divine Orbs** (value rerolls)
- Use: reroll existing mod values (without changing mods)
- Drop rate: extremely rare (1 per 10,000 monsters)
- Value: 120-150 chaos each
- Primary use: perfect-roll creation

### 2.2 Specialized Currency

**Essence Orbs**:
- Use: apply special stat to item
- Types: 50+ different essences
- Value: 1-20 chaos each depending on rarity
- Crafting: enables specific stat guarantees

**Fossil Orbs**:
- Use: apply special mod weight shifts to item
- Types: 30+ types (each shifts different mods)
- Value: 2-10 chaos each
- Crafting: specialized builds focus on fossils

**Catalyst Orbs**:
- Use: upgrade quality of items beyond 20%
- Levels: 1-10 (10 = 50% quality total)
- Value: 5-50 chaos depending on level
- Crafting: enables gear optimization

### 2.3 Vaal Orbs (Corruption)

**Vaal Orb Mechanics**:
- Use: corrupt item (unknown outcome)
- Outcomes: 
  1. +1 gem level (best outcome, 30% chance)
  2. +1 gem quality (20% chance)
  3. Reroll random modifier (30% chance)
  4. Item destroyed (20% chance)

**Economic Impact**:
- Item value before: 10 chaos
- Item value after (successful): 50 chaos (5x premium)
- Expected value: (50×0.30) + (15×0.20) + (10×0.30) + 0 = 22 chaos
- Cost: 1 Vaal Orb = 1 chaos
- Profit expectation: 22 - 10 - 1 = 11 chaos per successful flip

## Part 3: Advanced Currency Farming

### 3.1 Farming Routes

**Route 1: Pure Chaos Farming**:
- Run tier 10-12 maps continuously
- Expected drops: 80 chaos per hour
- No specialization needed
- Profit: 80 chaos/hour (reference baseline)

**Route 2: Unique Farming**:
- Farm specific maps with unique drops
- Example: "Strand" drops Kaom's Heart
- Expected: 1 unique per 20 maps (5 hours)
- Profit if unique worth 200 chaos: 40 chaos/hour (breakeven)

**Route 3: Rare Item Flipping**:
- Farm rares, identify valuable bases
- Sell rares for 5-20 chaos each
- Expected volume: 20 rares/hour
- Average price: 10 chaos
- Profit: 200 chaos/hour (+ highest-value finds)

**Route 4: Currency Crafting**:
- Buy cheap blue items (1 chaos each)
- Use alteration orbs to craft mods
- Sell crafted rares for 5-30 chaos
- Expected: 10 crafted items/hour
- Profit: 100-200 chaos/hour (high variance)

**Route 5: Essence Farming**:
- Farm essences from mobs (drop when essence-modded rares die)
- Collect 10 of same essence type
- Upgrade to next tier (10 → 1 higher level essence)
- Sell high-tier essences (100+ chaos each)
- Profit: 200-300 chaos/hour (requires specialization)

### 3.2 Currency Flipping

**Strategy**:
1. Identify underpriced items/currency
2. Buy in bulk at discount
3. Wait for price to stabilize
4. Sell at standard rate

**Example Flip**:
- Exalted Orbs at season start: 100 chaos (new farmers, supply low)
- Exalted Orbs at day 7: 150 chaos (stabilized price)
- Profit: 50 chaos per orb × 10 orbs = 500 chaos profit

**Risks**:
- Prices might drop (loss on position)
- Capital tied up (can't farm during holding)
- Requires market knowledge (predict trends)

## Part 5: Buy Orders vs. Sell Orders

### 5.1 Instant Buy Orders

**Function**: Player sets "I want to buy X at Y chaos"

**Advantage**:
- Instant purchase (no waiting)
- Convenience premium (pay extra for speed)

**Pricing**:
- Standard item at 100 chaos: buy order at 105 chaos (5% premium)
- Exalted orb at 150 chaos: buy order at 160 chaos (6.7% premium)
- Effect: buy orders are price-setters for popular items

### 5.2 Sell Orders

**Function**: Player sets "I want to sell X at Y chaos"

**Advantage**:
- Flexible pricing (can undercut competitors)
- Patient waiting (get exact price wanted)

**Pricing**:
- Exalted at 150 chaos: sell order at 148 (slight undercut for faster sale)
- Bulk currency: sell in bulk with discount (buy 50 = -5% per orb)

### 5.3 Price Discovery

**Market Dynamics**:
1. Buy orders and sell orders overlap
2. Transactions happen at mid-point price
3. New price becomes standard
4. Next buyer/seller references that price

**Example Transaction**:
- Buy order: 148 chaos for exalted
- Sell order: 152 chaos for exalted
- Trade at: 150 chaos (negotiated midpoint)
- New market price: 150 chaos reference

## Part 7: Tax Systems and Market Fees

### 7.1 (Hypothetical) Inflation Control

If game had auction house with fees:
- Buy listing fee: 1% of purchase price
- Sell fee: 2% of sale price
- Effect: removes currency from economy

**Example Trade**:
- Sale price: 100 chaos
- Seller fee: 2 chaos (paid to game)
- Buyer fee: 1 chaos (paid to game)
- Net currency removed: 3 chaos

**Inflation Control**:
- 1,000 trades per league = 3,000 chaos removed
- Prevents runaway inflation
- Stabilizes endgame economy

### 7.2 Current System (No Fees)

Path of Exile 2 currently has NO transaction fees:
- Direct player trading only
- No fees, no taxes, no automatic deduction
- Economy is purely supply/demand
