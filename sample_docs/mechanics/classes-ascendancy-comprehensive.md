# Path of Exile 2: Complete Class and Ascendancy Guide

## Part 1: Core Classes Overview

### 1.1 Warrior Class

**Starting Stats**: High physical damage, high life, low spell scaling

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Physical damage 30% higher than other classes at same gear
- Life pool +200 per level compared to other classes
- Ascendancy provides frenzy charge generation (10% more attack speed per charge, max 3 charges = 30% bonus)

**Ascendancy Path - Berserker**:
- Passive Grant 1: +50% attack damage, frenzy on kill
- Passive Grant 2: +2 red sockets (gem flexibility)
- Unique Mechanic: Rage generation (1 per 5% overkill damage, max 50 stacks = +50% damage)
- Build Application: Physical attack builds (Earthquake, Cleave, Reave)
- Scaling: aggressive offense, moderate defense (requires kiting)

**Item Synergies**:
- Kaom's Heart (500 flat life): mandatory unique
- Marohi Erqi (300 pDPS 2H mace): signature weapon
- Daresso's Salute (unique boots): enables frenzy charge uptime

**Play Style**: Face-tank low-damage enemies, kite high-threat bosses, maintain frenzy uptime.

### 1.2 Knight Class

**Starting Stats**: High armor/defenses, moderate physical damage, lowest spell scaling

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Armor +50% compared to other classes
- Life pool scales better with gear investment
- Ascendancy provides stagger mitigation (reduce stagger duration, increase poise)

**Ascendancy Path - Paladin**:
- Passive Grant 1: +30% physical damage, +200 armor
- Passive Grant 2: +50% block chance (50% of hits are negated)
- Unique Mechanic: Holy Shield (defensive aura, +50% resistances)
- Build Application: Tanky physical builds (Cyclone, block-scaling)
- Scaling: defensive first, offense second

**Item Synergies**:
- Unbreakable keystone (1,200 poise + immunity): enables face-tanking
- Shield with high armor: mandatory for block builds
- Kaom's Roots (unique boots): mandatory for flinch immunity

**Play Style**: Face-tank everything, block incoming damage, counterattack during recovery.

### 1.3 Ranger Class

**Starting Stats**: High evasion, moderate physical damage, low life

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Evasion +50% compared to other classes
- Movement speed +20% (enables kiting)
- Ascendancy provides projectile bonuses (+2 projectiles, +100% accuracy)

**Ascendancy Path - Deadeye**:
- Passive Grant 1: +2 projectiles (massive multiplier for projectile builds)
- Passive Grant 2: +100% projectile accuracy (guarantees hit without scaling)
- Unique Mechanic: Avatar of the Hunt (movement speed per frenzy charge, 10% per charge)
- Build Application: Physical projectile builds (bow attacks, spells)
- Scaling: offense through projectile scaling, defense through mobility

**Item Synergies**:
- Kaom's Roots (unique boots): mandatory for flinch immunity
- Bow with high physical DPS: enables projectile builds
- Movement speed gear: stacks multiplicatively

**Play Style**: Kite at maximum range, unleash projectiles, maintain distance permanently.

### 1.4 Shadow Class

**Starting Stats**: Moderate physical damage, high evasion, low life, moderate spell scaling

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Critical strike chance +30% compared to other classes
- Evasion + physical damage hybrid (enables conversion builds)
- Ascendancy provides poison/chaos scaling

**Ascendancy Path - Assassin**:
- Passive Grant 1: +100% critical multiplier (doubles crit damage)
- Passive Grant 2: +30% poison chance on crit
- Unique Mechanic: Poison stacking (multiple poisons per target, each deals damage)
- Build Application: Physical-to-chaos conversion (Poison builds, chaos DoT)
- Scaling: crit multiplier + poison stacking

**Item Synergies**:
- Lycosidae (unique shield, guaranteed hit): enables crit builds
- Daggers with high crit: scales crit multiplier
- Poison-scaling supports: mandatory for poison builds

**Play Style**: Achieve 100% crit, poison massive stacks, hide in shadows (aesthetic).

### 1.5 Witch Class

**Starting Stats**: High spell damage, highest mana, lowest armor/life

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Spell damage +40% compared to other classes
- Mana pool +300 per level
- Ascendancy provides spell-specific bonuses (spell level, ailment scaling)

**Ascendancy Path - Sorcerer**:
- Passive Grant 1: +1 spell level (+30% spell damage equivalent)
- Passive Grant 2: +40% elemental damage
- Unique Mechanic: Spell specialization (spells gain additional features: Fireball shoots 2x projectiles, etc.)
- Build Application: Pure spell builds (Fireball, Incinerate, Spell Echo)
- Scaling: spell multipliers (levels, damage, specialization)

**Item Synergies**:
- Crown of the Inquisitor (unique helm, +1 spell level): mandatory for damage
- Wand + Shield with spell scaling: enables spell weapon builds
- Mana-scaling gear: sustains expensive spell gems

**Play Style**: Spam spells from safety, maintain mana pool, leverage spell specialization.

### 1.6 Templar Class

**Starting Stats**: High spell damage, high life, moderate armor/evasion

**Archetype Strengths**:
- Spell damage + life hybrid (balanced offense/defense)
- Mana + life scaling (enables hybrid builds)
- Ascendancy provides hybrid bonuses

**Ascendancy Path - Hierophant**:
- Passive Grant 1: +100 mana per energy shield (enables mana scaling)
- Passive Grant 2: +50% spell damage, +50% mana regeneration
- Unique Mechanic: Ritual power (spells gain conditional bonuses in combat)
- Build Application: Mana-scaling spells (Archmage supports, Mind Over Matter)
- Scaling: hybrid offense/defense through mana

**Item Synergies**:
- Tukohama's Fortress (unique shield, +150 mana): mandatory for mana builds
- Astramentis (unique amulet, +40 all attributes): balances requirements
- Mana-scaling supports: Archmage (+3 spell damage per 100 mana)

**Play Style**: Scale mana defensively, use mana pool as pseudo-life, balance offense/defense.

## Part 2: Ascendancy Specializations

### 2.1 Aggressive Ascendancies

**Berserker** (Warrior):
- +50% attack damage, frenzy generation
- Rage mechanic: scales to +50% bonus at full stacks
- Best for: physical attack scaling (Earthquake, Cleave)
- Requires: sustain through leech or tank high DPS

**Deadeye** (Ranger):
- +2 projectiles (+200% damage multiplier for projectile builds)
- +100% accuracy (negates accuracy scaling)
- Best for: projectile-heavy builds (bow, spell echo)
- Requires: high projectile count (6+ base)

**Assassin** (Shadow):
- +100% critical multiplier (doubles crit damage)
- +30% poison chance on crit
- Best for: crit-scaling or poison builds
- Requires: high crit chance (80%+ to function)

### 2.2 Defensive Ascendancies

**Paladin** (Knight):
- +50% block chance (50% of hits blocked)
- +30% physical damage (scaling bonus)
- Best for: tank builds (Cyclone + block)
- Requires: shield investment (50%+ block from gear)

**Templar Ascendant** (generic):
- Passive life nodes (easier pathing)
- Flexibility (choose from multiple ascendancies)
- Best for: hybrid builds, first-time players
- Requires: 3-4 ascendancy passive allocations

### 2.3 Specialized Ascendancies

**Sorcerer** (Witch):
- +1 spell level, +40% elemental damage
- Spell specialization (unique mechanic per spell)
- Best for: pure spell builds (Fireball, Incinerate)
- Requires: high spell gem count

**Hierophant** (Templar):
- +100 mana per ES, +50% mana regen
- Ritual power (conditional bonuses)
- Best for: mana-scaling builds (Mind Over Matter, Archmage)
- Requires: 300%+ mana investment

## Part 3: Ascendancy Allocation Strategy

### 3.1 First Ascendancy Allocation

When entering the Labyrinth for the first time (Act 3):
1. Pick starting node (40% bonus in specific domain)
2. Choose 1 connector node (enables adjacent nodes)
3. Total: 2 nodes for 40% + synergy bonus

**Recommended first allocation by class**:
- Berserker: pick Rage generator first (enables frenzy → damage snowball)
- Paladin: pick block generator first (enables defense scaling)
- Deadeye: pick +projectile first (enables multi-projectile builds)

### 3.2 Second Ascendancy Allocation

In cruel labyrinth (Act 6):
1. Pick adjacent node to first (synergy stack)
2. Choose secondary offensive or defensive node
3. Total: 2-3 nodes for 80-120% synergy bonuses

### 3.3 Third and Fourth Allocations

In merciless and eternal labyrinths (post-acts):
1. Complete ascendancy specialization (all 4-6 nodes in specific tree)
2. Unlock unique mechanic (keystone passive)
3. Total: full ascendancy = 200%+ power bonus

## Part 4: Class-Specific Gearing Recommendations

### 4.1 Warrior Gear Priorities

**Weapon**: Prioritize base damage (300+ pDPS 2H or 200+ 1H)
**Body Armor**: Prioritize life (+100 flat minimum) + armor (+100 minimum)
**Boots**: Prioritize movement speed (+25%) and flinch immunity
**Defenses**: Armor > resistances (warrior-specific bonus)

**Stat Targets (endgame)**:
- Physical damage: 1,500+ (combined gear + tree)
- Armor: 8,000+ (stacks with high HP)
- Life: 3,500+ (face-tank requirement)
- Attack speed: 1.8+ (frenzy uptime)

### 4.2 Ranger Gear Priorities

**Weapon**: Bow with high projectile damage (200+ pDPS)
**Body Armor**: Prioritize evasion (200+ evasion) and life (+80 flat)
**Boots**: Mandatory: +25%+ movement speed (enables kiting)
**Defenses**: Evasion > armor (ranger-specific bonus)

**Stat Targets (endgame)**:
- Physical damage: 1,000+ projectile DPS
- Evasion: 2,500+ (stacks with dodge passives)
- Life: 3,000+ (fragile build requires care)
- Movement speed: 150%+ (maintain distance)

### 4.3 Witch Gear Priorities

**Weapon**: Wand with spell scaling (100+ spell damage)
**Body Armor**: Prioritize ES (150+ energy shield) and life (+80)
**Offhand**: Shield with spell scaling and ES (enables spell builds)
**Defenses**: Energy Shield > evasion (hybrid defense)

**Stat Targets (endgame)**:
- Spell damage: 1,000+ (from all sources)
- Energy shield: 1,500+ (sustains spell builds)
- Life: 2,500+ (spell builds have low inherent armor)
- Mana: 400+ (sustains expensive gems)

## Conclusion

Class choice determines archetype viability:
- **Warriors**: physical attackers (tanky, high damage)
- **Knights**: block-based tanks (defensive, steady)
- **Rangers**: projectile/kite builds (mobile, evasion-based)
- **Shadows**: crit/poison builds (specialized, damage-oriented)
- **Witches**: pure spell builds (highest offense, low defense)
- **Templars**: hybrid builds (balanced, flexible)

Choosing the right class for your desired playstyle is the first optimization step in Path of Exile 2.
